Not Every Thief Runs Away

Not all thefts happen in darkness. Some almost happen in daylight—inside the trembling heart of an honest man.

By PaddyMay 3, 20252 min read

Not all thefts happen in darkness. Some almost happen in daylight—inside the trembling heart of an honest man.

Meet Selvamuthu Goundar, a grocery clerk in Dindigul, Tamil Nadu. Thirty-five years of service. Not a raise. Not a bonus. Not even basic dignity. His employer thrived. He survived.

One night, something snapped.

A customer mocked him—

“You act like it’s your shop. No wonder you’re poor.”

It wasn’t just an insult. It was the final drop in a cup overflowing with humiliation. That night, Selvamuthu counted the day’s cash—₹38,450—and packed it in a tiffin box. He wasn’t stealing. He was “taking back” what the world owed him.

Or so he told himself.

🛤️ The Train of Temptation

At Dindigul Junction, he bought a one-way ticket to Rameswaram. From there, he’d escape to Sri Lanka.

Just as the train prepared to leave, a familiar voice called out— Subbaiya, now a police officer, slapped him on the back.

“Enna machan! Off to elope with a Ceylon queen?”

Selvamuthu smiled, barely. Lied about a cousin’s funeral. He boarded the train.

But as the train moved, so did something inside him.

He saw his wife—shamed. His children—mocked. His name—rotting.

And then, something louder than fear spoke.

He pulled the emergency chain.

🏃♂️ The Return to Dharma

Before sunrise, he was back at the store. He placed the tiffin box in front of his stunned employer.

“I packed it to steal, ayya. But I couldn’t. I saw my children’s future in shame. I’d rather be poor than be a thief.”

There was no applause. No reward. Not even his family knew what he had resisted.

But that night—on a torn mattress, under a creaking fan— Selvamuthu Goundar slept like a free man.

🧘♂️ Karma Yoga in Real Life

In the Bhagavad Gita, Krishna speaks not just to warriors, but to all of us—clerks, parents, managers, workers.

Let’s revisit his words from Chapter 3: Karma Yoga:

“Not by refraining from action does one attain freedom… Nor by inaction alone is perfection achieved.” (Gita 3.4)

Selvamuthu tried to escape. But it wasn’t escape that freed him. It was return. Not to work. But to truth.

“He who controls action outwardly but dwells mentally on desire is called a hypocrite.” (Gita 3.6)

He didn’t just return the money. He returned to his Dharma.

🪔 The Real Message

This isn’t a story about crime. It’s a story about conscience.

Karma Yoga isn’t perfection. It’s not about renouncing the world. It’s about choosing rightly—even when no one is watching.

Selvamuthu wasn’t a sage. He wasn’t a CEO. He was a grocery clerk.

But in that moment, he was a warrior.

And that night, he slept better than any king.

💬 Reflection

In a world obsessed with performance, reward, and visibility— are we forgetting the quiet, invisible victories?

Can we find peace not by achieving more… but by doing right?

Let Selvamuthu Goundar remind us:

🟤 Honesty is not loud. 🟤 Dharma is not glamorous. 🟤 But peace? Peace is priceless.
