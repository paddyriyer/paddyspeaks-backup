ERPs Will Soon Be RIPs: The Funeral March Has Already Begun

Old Way: Tableau dashboard cluttered with 12 filters, charts no one reads.

By PaddyAugust 17, 20255 min read

Future of Enterprise Software

1. The CEO Query in Action

  • Old Way: Tableau dashboard cluttered with 12 filters, charts no one reads.

  • Future Way: A simple search bar on screen: “Show Q2 revenue vs. projections by region.” → AI instantly generates a clean chart + narrative insight.

2. The Death of Dashboards

  • Old Way: Homepage with 50+ dashboards listed like tombstones.

  • Future Way: A chat window with a CEO asking: “Why are churn rates rising in Asia?” → AI agent returns 1 chart + 2 bullet explanations.

3. Pipeline Collapse → Cognitive Orchestration

  • Old Way: Complicated ETL flow diagram with 50 arrows and data lake boxes.

  • Future Way: Three glowing AI agents labeled Intent → Data → Visualization, instantly coordinating.

4. Cloud Shrinks

  • Old Way: AWS/Azure/GCP bills in the tens of thousands, filled with mysterious services.

  • Future Way: Local/agent-based query that pulls distributed answers, cost = near zero.

5. CIO to CEO Direct Line

  • Old Way: CIO presenting 40-slide PowerPoint in a boardroom.

  • Future Way: CEO asks laptop/phone: “What’s the margin outlook if we cut prices 5%?” → Agents deliver scenario simulation in real-time.

For 40 years, enterprises have been paying protection money. Bloated software vendors sold you “innovation,” but what you really bought was digital feudalism.

  • SAP built empires of complexity.

  • Oracle taxed you like a medieval king.

  • Salesforce turned every click into a subscription fee.

  • Tableau convinced you dashboards were “insights.”

☁️ The Cloud Mafia Satire

  • AWS = Always Waiting for Surprises

  • Azure = “Assure you’ll need support”

  • GCP = Google’s Cash Printer

Together they’re not cloud providers. They’re casinos with dashboards. Spin the wheel, pull the lever, hope your CFO doesn’t faint.

🕴️ The TPM Comedy Club

  • TPMs were the priests of ambiguity. They spoke in tongues: “We need alignment on the dependencies to unblock the stakeholders for Phase 0 readiness.” Translation: “I booked another meeting.”

  • Their entire survival strategy: inflate complexity, delay clarity, thrive in fog. Complexity was their currency; ambiguity was their oxygen. Now? AI agents cut through fog like headlights. The priesthood is over.

Meanwhile, armies of engineers padded the bill with mammoths of dashboards, dinosaur codebases, and labyrinthine ETL pipelines—half of which exist not for business, but for job security.

But the new age isn’t knocking. It’s blowing the doors off.

⚠️ CIOs: Time’s Up

CIOs and their team lived off complexity. Their currency: acronym soup (ERP, CRM, DW, ETL, API, Data Lake, Mesh). But CEOs want clarity, not jargon. The new model is brutally simple:

  • CEO asks.

  • AI agents swarm: one deciphers intent, one fetches data, one builds the narrative.

  • Seconds later: Boom—a decision, not a dashboard. No analysts, no alignment meetings, no “please refresh the portal.” Just business intelligence at the speed of thought.

⚡ Punchlines for the Lazy Enterprise

  • 🦣 Mammoths of Dashboards → Extinct. No one wants to scroll through 50 charts when an agent gives the answer in plain English.

  • 🗂️ “Reporting Portals” → Digital graveyards. Built once, visited never.

  • 🛠️ Complex Codebases → Written like mazes so engineers stay employed. AI will read them, refactor them, then fire their authors.

  • ⏳ ETL Pipelines → The hamster wheels of IT. Agents won’t “extract, transform, load.” They’ll understand, reason, answer.

  • 👔 CIO Empires → Built on fear and acronyms. By 2030, the CEO will talk to AI agents directly. No middle kingdom required.

🚀 The Wrecking Crew Is Already Inside

Meet the new vanguard: Glean , Perplexity Grok OpenAI ChatGPT AI Anthropic - Claude and lining up behind them: Mistral, Cohere, Hugging Face, LangChain/LlamaIndex, Replit Ghostwriter, MosaicML, Together AI. Don’t call them “tools”—they’re demolition squads for enterprise bloat.

By 2030, here’s the reality shock:

  • Software dies. Intelligence emerges. “Enterprise software” will sound as quaint as “horseless carriage.”

  • Dashboards evaporate. Why click, filter, and pray when you can just ask and converse?

  • ETL & Pipelines implode. Agents orchestrate cognition, not alphabet soup.

  • Cloud shrinks to fit. Why ship oceans of data to a warehouse, when federated AI agents process at the edge, in real time?🌌 The Future Enterprise OS: Search. Converse. Think.

By the 2030s, your “enterprise stack” won’t be SAP or Salesforce. It’ll be three things:

  • Search: Ask in plain English.

  • Conversation: Refine, interrogate, and debate your AI co-pilot.

  • Cognition: Agents synthesize and resolve messy data—live, invisible, everywhere.

No one will care where the data lives—no logos, no portals, no silos. Ambient intelligence replaces the stack. Answer, not infrastructure, rules.

🚀 The Real KO

Enterprise IT has long thrived on delays disguised as processes and complexity disguised as value. But AI agents don’t need job security. They don’t need “alignment meetings.” They don’t need six months to “scope a dashboard.”

They just answer. In seconds. Every time.

🧠 What’s in the New-Gen Stack?

  • Knowledge & Search: Glean, Perplexity

  • Reasoning & Dialogue: ChatGPT, Claude, Grok, Cohere, Mistral

  • Orchestration (ETL killer): LangChain, LlamaIndex

  • Code & Automation: Replit Ghostwriter, OpenAI/Claude-Code, MosaicML

  • Infra: Together AI, MosaicML, Open-weight Mistral & Hugging Face Call it what it is: A brain, not a building.

🥋 Bruce-Lee-Style Combos

  • “Dashboards = Data cemeteries. Ask instead.”

  • “If it required a steering committee or bunch of TPMs, it’s too slow for 2030.”

  • “Conversational agents kill report bottlenecks.”

  • “Stop governing data at the expense of action.”

  • “If your portal is front-page, your company is history.”

🧱 What You Can’t Automate (Yet)

Even when comets hit, gravity stays:

  • Truth: Source lineage, audit trails

  • Trust: Permissions, privacy, compliance

  • Taste: Human judgment, scenario framing, ethics

  • Triage: Know what to automate, escalate, or ignore AI melts the machinery—humans steer the meaning.

🛠️ The 90-Day Playbook to Jumpstart 2030

  • Kill five dashboards—replace them with one agent answering live questions.

  • Enforce “question to answer” SLAs—think seconds, not sprints.

  • Pilot agent-orchestrated queries between “impossible” siloed systems.

  • Publicly retire one legacy report each week—demonstrate its replacement with conversational AI.

  • Challenge execs: Go portal-free for a week. If it needs more than chat, it’s not ready.

🧨 Final Shot

Enterprise giants strutted around like dinosaurs, building pyramids of dashboards and pipelines as monuments to their own job security. But pyramids don’t pivot. Dinosaurs don’t sprint.

When the comet of AI slams down, they won’t “transform.” They’ll fossilize—beautiful, expensive relics for the corporate museum tour.

The 2030s aren’t here to “evolve enterprise software.” 👉 They’re here to bury it with last month’s invoices.
